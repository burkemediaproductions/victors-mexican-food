A family dream, built over generations.

Victor’s story begins in El Malacate, a village on the border of Zacatecas and Jalisco, where family, work, and hospitality shaped the future of the restaurant.

The story

A childhood dream that never went away.

As a child in a small town, Victor saw a chef on television and told his mother he would become one one day. Years later, after hard work and patience, he earned his first chef position and eventually opened his own restaurant.

More than 33 years in the U.S.
Opened December 15, 2018
Moved to prime location in 2024

Family names at the heart of the story

Carmen, Victor Jr., Eduardo, and Paloma are part of the family-centered spirit behind the restaurant.

Camino Real roots

Where commerce, travel, and hope crossed paths.

Victor’s grandfather ran a small store in the Sierra where arrieros traveling by horse would stop, trade, and rest. That memory of service and enterprise lives on in the restaurant’s identity today.

A life beyond the kitchen

White doves, family, and community.

In his free time, Victor raises homing doves and pigeons and is connected to White Doves for You in the Coachella Valley, adding another layer of care, tradition, and local connection to the story.
